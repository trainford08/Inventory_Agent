Variant B — page-specific (secondary) sidebar leftmost, app (primary) sidebar inside, content right. Reads: what I'm doing right now → the app around it → my work. Less conventional, but puts the active-work scaffold closest to the screen edge where reviewers' eyes live.
  • Primary nav becomes visually nested — people may read it as secondary-to-the-page even though it's global.
  • Same column widths as Variant A (~ 260 + 248 + remaining).

Trade-offs side-by-side

Variant A

Primary → Secondary → Main

Pro: matches every major app users already know. "Global nav on the outside" is muscle memory.

Pro: when the user leaves the review (clicks a different app-nav item), their finger travels from the far-left edge — a familiar motion.

Con: page-specific scaffold is one column in from the edge.

Variant B

Secondary → Primary → Main

Pro: review progress sits at the screen edge — the Champion's daily scaffold is "closest".

Con: breaks convention — the global nav looks nested, which implies subordinate scope.

Con: harder to learn for new users; they'll often try to close the "inner" bar thinking it's page-scoped.

Say which variant to build, or ask for a third option. If the intent behind "secondary to the left of the primary" was Variant A (conventional order), that's what I'd recommend.
